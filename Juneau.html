Juneau Capital of Alaska

    Juneau Facts

  1. In 1906, the capital of Alaska was moved from Sitka to Juneau.
  2. Two tribes of the Tlingit Alaska Native people lived and fished along the Gastineau Channel near Juneau.
  3. Juneau is big, with a total area of 3,255 square miles.
  4. The most famous glacier near Juneau is the Mendenhall Glacier.
  5. On the winter solstice, there are just 6 hours and 25 minutes of daylight.

Wild Life

Black-tailed Deer: These graceful animals can be seen grazing in the nearby meadows and forests.

Humpback Whales: Visitors can take a tour on a boat to get up close and personal with these majestic creatures

Bears: Visitors should always exercise caution when viewing bears, as they are wild animals and can be unpredictable.
